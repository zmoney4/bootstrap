What is a Pizza?

pizza-noun

piz·​za | pēt-sə

a dish made typically of flattened bread dough spread with a savory mixture usually including tomatoes and cheese and often other toppings and baked — called also a pizza pie

Rating Criteria

#1 Rated Pizza Analyst by Bon Appetit, Catalyst, TASTE, Disney Food Blog, Eating Well, Edible Communities, Epicure & Culture, Eater, & Forbes

Crust

A good slice of pizza starts with good dough. When examining your pizza, make sure to check the bottom and the crust. The bottom should be well done to offer a nice crisp bite, and the crust should be toasted on the outside but fluffy and light on the inside. That’s a good indication that the dough was prepared properly and allowed enough time to rise and rest.

Sauce

A pizzeria that’s serious about its pizza will mix its own sauce. A good way to taste the sauce is by either requesting extra on your pizza or ordering a side with your breadsticks. You should enjoy savory and bright notes rather than sweet or dull.

Cheese

The most commonly used cheese on pizza is mozzarella, because it melts beautifully without turning oily or lumpy. Cheeses such as feta, haloumi and aged gouda are tasty toppings, but they don't melt as well as mozzarella. Low-moisture mozzarella in particular has great melt and stretch.

Toppings

There is a lot that can go wrong with the toppings on a pizza. They need to be cooked to perfection – so not undercooked and not overcooked to the point of being soggy or burnt to a crisp. The toppings should also work well together to create a good flavor. And you don’t want a single topping to overpower the flavor of the rest of the toppings.

Price

Prices for meat, cheese and certain grains are on the rise, making pizzas more expensive to make. Food input costs aren't the only cause for concern. Higher labor costs in the restaurant industry are also expected to persist this year. A good pie with a reasonable price is unbeatable
